# Imagery

The Svenska Spel imagery guidelines featuring our photography style, composition principles, and visual storytelling approach.

---

## Philosophy

**"Together we create a greater feeling"**

The overarching idea for our images is to capture how it feels to be part of Svenska Spel with a broad emotional range. Our imagery style is authentic, positive, and close.

**Core Principles:**
- **Subjects that help us speak about the three foundations:**
  - **Spelglädje** (Joy of gaming) - The excitement and fun
  - **Omtanke** (Care) - Responsibility and support
  - **Ge tillbaka** (Give back) - Community contribution
- **Cohesive image style** across business areas with variation in subjects and context
- **People-focused** - More emphasis on people, less on products and features

---

## Image Characteristics

Our imagery has distinct visual characteristics that make it recognizable as Svenska Spel.

### 1. Close Perspective

**Approach:**
A wide lens and close perspective make you feel like you're part of the situation being depicted.

**Purpose:**
- Creates intimacy and connection
- We meet our audience at eye level
- Viewer becomes part of the moment

**Implementation:**
- Use wide-angle lenses for environmental context
- Get physically close to subjects
- Capture perspectives that invite the viewer in

---

### 2. Natural Warm Light

**Lighting Style:**
The light in our images is natural, preferably with a clear direction that creates dynamics and contrasts.

**Color Temperature:**
- Warm color tones
- Natural light sources
- Golden hour quality when possible

**Purpose:**
- Creates warmth and approachability
- Adds energy and dimension
- Reinforces authentic feeling

**Avoid:**
- Flat, even lighting
- Artificial studio lighting (unless styled naturally)
- Cool or harsh tones

---

### 3. Red Details

**Brand Integration:**
Our red brand colors should be present in our images.

**Why:**
Makes it clearer that the images come from us - strengthens brand recognition.

**Implementation:**
- Look for natural red elements in scenes
- Red clothing or accessories
- Environmental red details
- Vinnarkuben presence when appropriate
- Red in composition accents

**Balance:**
- Should feel natural, not forced
- Can be subtle touches or bold statements
- Part of the scene, not added artificially

---

### 4. Authentic Situations

**Authenticity:**
The situations we depict don't need to be perfect. The most important thing is that it feels real.

**Where We Capture:**
- Everyday life across the country
- Living rooms
- On the bus
- In the locker room
- Real environments where people actually are

**Purpose:**
- Creates relatability
- Shows genuine moments
- Represents all of Sweden

**Avoid:**
- Overly staged or posed scenarios
- Generic stock photo feeling
- Artificial perfection
- Situations that feel inauthentic

---

### 5. Sense of Energy

**Movement and Vitality:**
We are a company in constant motion and with our connection to sports, it's natural that our images have a clear sense of energy and pace.

**Implementation:**
- Action moments
- Dynamic compositions
- Movement blur when appropriate
- Energetic body language
- Moments of anticipation

**Purpose:**
- Reflects our dynamic brand
- Connects to sports and gaming excitement
- Creates engagement

---

## Image Categories

Our imagery is organized into distinct categories, each serving specific communication needs.

### Lifestyle Images

**Priority:** This is our most important image category and makes up the majority of our images.

**Focus:**
We focus on our players' experiences and depict them in various situations with high recognition.

**Emotional Capture:**
We want our images of people to capture emotions:
- The tension before the match
- The community on the stands
- The feeling before the river card
- The dream of the jackpot
- The euphoria of getting 13 right (correct predictions)

**Subjects:**
- Real people in real situations
- Genuine emotions and reactions
- Shared experiences
- Moments of connection
- Gaming and sports contexts

**Purpose:**
- Creates emotional connection with audience
- Shows relatable experiences
- Demonstrates the human side of gaming

---

### Product Images

**Usage:** Use sparingly

**Approach:**
When we show products or services, we can do it in devices like a phone or watch, or with individual modules. By placing them against photos or text, we create a living and energetic expression.

**Important Note:**
These images should be used sparingly. We try to have more focus on people and less focus on products and features.

**Implementation:**
- Devices in context (phones, tablets, smartwatches)
- UI modules and interface elements
- Placed against photography or overlaid with text
- Integrated into lifestyle scenes when possible

**Avoid:**
- Pure product shots without context
- Making products the hero over people
- Overusing product-focused imagery

---

### Purchased Sports Images

**Context:**
When we purchase this type of image with actual athletes, there are clear rules we must adhere to.

**Restrictions:**
- ✗ Cannot show team emblems or sponsors on jerseys
- ✗ Cannot show specific players, their names, or faces

**Strategy:**
Despite these restrictions, it's important to strive to make these images feel like our own.

**Implementation:**
- Look for new angles and situations
- Find unique perspectives that:
  - Make images more unique to us
  - Fulfill the rules about what can be shown
- Adjust images in contrast and color to harmonize with our own photography when possible

**Goal:**
Balance regulatory compliance with maintaining our visual identity.

---

### Images of Our Athletes

**Approach:**
Since our national team stars appear in images in many situations, we strive to photograph them in our imagery style so it's visible that these are our images.

**Strategy:**
- Avoid generic images
- Find our own situations and angles
- Own the images more clearly through distinctive perspective

**Key Elements:**
- Capture both emotions and community
- When we have rights to show faces and names, do so - makes it easy to see who's in the image
- Show relatability and personality

**Beyond the Sport:**
We also portrait our sponsored athletes in situations outside of sports to:
- Further leverage our sponsorships
- Show the relationship between them and Svenska Spel
- Humanize athletes beyond their sport

**Implementation:**
- Behind-the-scenes moments
- Personal stories
- Community engagement
- Training and preparation
- Celebration and emotion

## Photography Best Practices

### Composition

**Eye Level Perspective:**
- Meet subjects at their eye level
- Creates equality and connection
- Avoid looking down or up unless intentional

**Wide Lens for Context:**
- Capture environment and atmosphere
- Show where moments happen
- Create sense of place

**Rule of Thirds:**
- Position subjects dynamically
- Create visual interest
- Guide viewer's eye

---

### Lighting

**Natural Light Priority:**
- Shoot during golden hour when possible
- Use window light for indoor shots
- Maintain warm tones

**Directional Light:**
- Create depth and dimension
- Show texture and form
- Add drama when appropriate

**Avoid:**
- Flat overhead lighting
- Harsh midday sun without diffusion
- Mixed color temperatures

---

### Color and Tone

**Warm Color Palette:**
- Enhance warm tones in post-processing
- Maintain natural skin tones
- Subtle adjustments, not heavy filters

**Contrast:**
- Use natural light to create contrast
- Don't flatten images in editing
- Maintain dynamic range

**Red Integration:**
- Look for red in the scene
- Can enhance red elements subtly
- Don't add red artificially via heavy color grading

---

## Editing Guidelines

### Post-Processing Approach

**Natural Enhancement:**
- Enhance what's already there
- Don't transform the image
- Maintain authenticity

**Adjustments:**
- Contrast and color balance
- Exposure correction
- Slight warm tone enhancement
- Sharpening for clarity

**Avoid:**
- Heavy filters or presets
- Artificial color shifts
- Over-saturation
- Excessive contrast that looks digital
- Fake bokeh or blur effects

---

### Consistency

**Harmonize Images:**
When using purchased sports images, adjust contrast and color so they harmonize with our own photography.

**Batch Consistency:**
- Use similar editing approach across image sets
- Maintain cohesive look within campaigns
- Create visual continuity

---

## Usage Guidelines

### Do's

**✓ Capture Real Moments:**
- Authentic emotions and reactions
- Genuine situations
- Real environments

**✓ Show People:**
- Prioritize human subjects
- Capture emotions and connections
- Show community and shared experiences

**✓ Use Natural Light:**
- Golden hour shooting
- Window light for indoors
- Directional natural light

**✓ Include Red Details:**
- Natural red elements in scene
- Brand color integration
- Vinnarkuben when appropriate

**✓ Create Energy:**
- Action and movement
- Dynamic compositions
- Moments of anticipation

**✓ Show Diversity:**
- Represent all of Sweden
- Different ages and backgrounds
- Various contexts and situations

---

### Don'ts

**✗ Generic Stock Photos:**
- Avoid typical stock photo feeling
- No overly staged scenarios
- Don't use generic business/lifestyle stock

**✗ Artificial Situations:**
- Avoid obviously posed shots
- No fake or forced scenarios
- Don't create situations that feel inauthentic

**✗ Product-Heavy Focus:**
- Don't prioritize products over people
- Avoid pure product photography
- Don't overuse device shots

**✗ Cold or Harsh Lighting:**
- Avoid cool color temperatures
- No harsh, unflattering light
- Don't use flat studio lighting

**✗ Heavy Post-Processing:**
- No artificial filters
- Avoid heavy color grading
- Don't over-edit to unrealistic results

**✗ Rules Violations:**
For purchased sports images:
- Never show team logos or sponsors
- Don't show identifiable players without rights
- Avoid content that violates licensing

## Image Formats and Specifications

### Aspect Ratios

Prepare images in multiple aspect ratios for different use cases:

**Common Ratios:**
- **16:9** - Video, web headers, digital displays
- **4:3** - Traditional formats, presentations
- **1:1** - Social media, Instagram, square formats
- **9:16** - Mobile, Stories, vertical video
- **3:2** - Photography standard

### Resolution

**Digital:**
- Minimum 1920px on longest side for web
- 72-96 DPI for screen use
- Optimize file size for web performance

**Print:**
- 300 DPI minimum
- Size appropriate for final print dimensions
- CMYK color mode for print production

## Accessibility Considerations

### Alt Text

Always provide meaningful alt text:
- Describe the scene and context
- Include relevant emotions and actions
- Mention key details that convey meaning
- Don't start with "Image of..."

### Contrast

When placing text over images:
- Ensure sufficient contrast (WCAG AA minimum)
- Use overlays or gradients when needed
- Test readability across devices

### Context

Images should:
- Make sense without surrounding text
- Convey meaning visually
- Support the message, not replace it
